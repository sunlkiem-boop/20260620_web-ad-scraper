## Product reality — get the format right

Aonic Complete is **not a powder and not a single pill.** It is a **dual-phase daily ritual: a 1.1 oz on-the-go liquid shot + 3 softgels + 1 capsule, in a patent-pending dual-chamber container.** The Big Idea is *"One powder can't do it all."* Don't depict it as a tub, scoop, or greens powder.

- Brand-approved category: **"Daily Essential Nutrition"** — never "multivitamin," "greens powder," or "foundational supplement."
- Editions: **HIS** and **HERS** (gender-specific formulas).

## Compliance reminders (binding — from the Product Book / offer brief)

These also live in the foundational docs; flagged here as a fast-reference. Any structure-function claim must carry the FDA disclaimer below.

- **FDA disclaimer (required on claims):** "These statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."
- Use **"Made in the USA with globally sourced ingredients"** — never "Made in USA" alone.
- Use **"scientifically studied dosages"** / "clinically studied dosages" — never "clinically effective."
- Use **"no pixie-dusting"** (a tribal signal for the AG1-churner buyer).
- Use **"We-Use-It-Ourselves Guarantee"** — never "money-back guarantee" (there is no refund policy).
- Use **"Brain Health & Function"** — never "focus."
- Use **"Immune Support"** — never "boost" or "immunity."
- Avoid **"balance hormones" / "hormone imbalance"** (especially the HERS funnel); avoid hype verbs "boost," "optimize," "unlock."
- **"No added sugar" is not Complete's lane** (that's Aonic Fuel/Revive) — don't use it here.
- Don't label competitors "a marketing company" in paid headlines (advertorial/organic body copy only).
