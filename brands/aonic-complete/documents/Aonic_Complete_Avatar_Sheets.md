# CROSS-PERSONA NOTES

Where the three Complete avatars converge — the universal anchors any single piece of copy can hit:

- **The drawer / kitchen counter pharmacy.** All three avatars have it. The "one product" promise lands universally — and the brand frame is *Daily Essential Nutrition in one beautiful on-the-go design,* not "the AG1 you should switch to."
- **AG1 disappointment shows up in three different ways.** The AG1 churner did the annualized math and quit on principle; Ben built his own stack around AG1 because AG1 didn't include vitamin D, omega-3, or the right B12; Chloe found AG1 "too earthy" and never made it past the first bag. Different surface complaints, same underlying truth: AG1 is a greens powder; Complete is a complete daily nutrient platform.
- **The annualized number ($1,188/year) is the single most copy-actionable price quote in the entire research corpus.** It works across avatars — but it's a critique of AG1's *value*, not a claim that Aonic is cheaper.
