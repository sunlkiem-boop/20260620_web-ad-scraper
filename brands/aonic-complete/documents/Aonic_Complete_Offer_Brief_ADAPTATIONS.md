## CRITICAL CORRECTIONS TO MY PRIOR WORK

Before adapting anything else, three things in the VOC dossier and avatar sheets need to be walked back, because the offer brief reveals product realities the public research couldn't see:

### Correction 1 — Pricing framing was wrong

**My dossier said:** Aonic Complete is "a fraction of AG1's price"; "we charge a third of that"; "you shouldn't have to pay $99/month for ingredients you can see."

**The brief reveals:** Aonic Complete is **$115/30-day supply** — *more* expensive monthly than AG1's $79 sub / $99 one-time.

**The actual value framing (per brief):** Complete consolidates **~$230/month of separate-product spend into one box**; **~$1,800/year savings vs. building the equivalent stack à la carte**. The brand is *not* competing with AG1 on price; it's competing with **the cost of a multi-supplement stack** that an AG1 user is *already augmenting* AG1 with (a fish oil, a vitamin D, a probiotic, a women's multi, etc.).

**Operational fix:**
- Drop "fraction of AG1's price" from any copy briefed off the dossier.
- Replace with: *"$115 replaces $230+ of separate bottles."* / *"More than AG1. Less than the stack you're building around AG1."*
- The Ben avatar's *"$1,188 a year for something I could approximate with a single greens powder for $300-360"* still works — but it's a critique of AG1, not a price claim Aonic should mirror. Aonic's pitch is "AG1 doesn't replace your stack. Complete does."

### Correction 2 — Aonic Complete is dual-format, not a single product

**My dossier and avatar sheets treated Complete as a single-format daily** (implicitly, a powder or capsule). The brief reveals it is a **dual-phase ritual: 1.1 oz liquid shot + 3 softgels + 1 capsule, in a patent-pending dual-chamber container.** This is the entire mechanism. Every avatar pain point, headline, and belief in this adaptation has to bend around it.

The Big Idea — *"One powder can't do it all."* — is the strongest single line in the brief and is the line every piece of creative should be testable against.

### Correction 3 — The 50+ healthspan buyer is Flow's audience, not Complete's

The brief explicitly notes Aonic Flow has its own (separate) brief for the blood-pressure / 50+ buyer. My third avatar — the older healthspan buyer / Centrum loyalist / spouse-influenced — belongs primarily to Flow, not Complete.

**For Complete specifically, the avatar set is:**
1. **AG1 Churner / Sophisticated Foundational-Supplement Shopper** (PRIMARY — built below)
2. **Chloe-leaning first-time foundational buyer** (SECONDARY → HERS funnel)
3. **Ben-leaning performance-adjacent foundational buyer** (SECONDARY → HIS funnel)

The healthspan-buyer avatar I wrote stays in the master research file for future Flow work but should not drive Complete creative.
