# i tried running only AI ads for 30 days (it printed lol)

## TL;DR
Mark spent ~$1M in one month running 100% AI-generated ads and hit $2.2M in sales. The core argument: AI collapsed the classic quality / speed / cost triangle, so the moat is no longer access to tools — it's the *thinking* behind each creative and the *systemization* of that thinking. Aim for a winning **message** that transmutes across formats, not a winning **ad**.

## Frameworks & models

- **The old trade-off triangle.** Pre-AI you picked any two of {quality, speed, cheap}; you couldn't have all three. AI gives you all three at once — which is why barrier to entry collapsed and competitor ad volume is exploding exponentially.
- **The AI non-negotiables.** Three prerequisites without which AI just generates faster slop:
  1. **Copy is the foundation of everything.** AI is a multiplier on your psychology research, not a replacement for it.
  2. **Funnel is a creative multiplier.** A rising-tide funnel lifts the floor and ceiling of every creative.
  3. **Right tech for each use case.** Image vs video vs animation each need different tools.
- **"I was just like you but worse."** UGC script framework: the creator presents a more extreme version of the customer's problem, then a resolution — letting the viewer think "if she could, I obviously can".
- **Native static principle = blend in + contrast.** Native statics blend in with the feed visually but use copy/imagery contrast (e.g. visceral medical-looking visuals) to stop the scroll. The creative is the scroll stopper; the copy does the selling.
- **Look backwards, not forwards.** Top ad formats from TV history (e.g. dropper-style supplement spots, infomercial structures) are now winning again on Meta. Twitter and IG marketing influencers are 6 months to multiple years behind.

## Key principles

- "The person who understands their customer best will destroy the person with better tools every single time."
- AI doesn't change human psychology — it multiplies your understanding of it. If your copy sucks, AI makes shitty ads faster and more of them.
- Aim for a **winning message** (transmutable across formats, hooks, creators) rather than chasing a winning ad.
- 60–80% of historical copywriting time goes to *research*, not writing. AI lets you compress this to ~30 minutes via the foundational docs SOP.
- Realism in AI is no longer a moat — everyone has it. Don't confine yourself to hyperrealistic UGC or VSLs; some of the best-performing creatives are stupid AI animations.
- Funnel diversity > creative diversity. A great funnel makes mediocre creatives profitable.
- One of the top 5 most powerful words in copy: **without** (e.g. "achieve X without Y" — used to discredit common solutions).

## Tactics & playbook steps

- **For images:** Nano Banana Pro via Higgsfield is sufficient — nothing else needed if prompting + reference images are good.
- **For video:** Image-to-video pipeline is the default. Generate the image in Nano Banana Pro, then animate via:
  - **Cling 3.0** — best quality/cost balance, better prompt adherence than Veo.
  - **Veo 3.1** — cheaper and faster, clip-length limits.
  - **Seedance 2.0** — most realistic but expensive and slow. Mark uses it only to *remake* top performers, never for testing.
- **Tweaking AI images:** Download from Higgsfield → Canva → Magic Layer to edit individual elements (text, color, fonts) without round-tripping to Nano Banana Pro. Free.
- **Prompting:** Don't write prompts manually. Speak via Whisper / Super Whisper, then have an LLM convert your speech into an image or image-to-video prompt.
- **Research before generation:** Always feed foundational docs (offer / avatar / research / beliefs) into Claude/ChatGPT before asking it to rewrite competitor ads. Skipping research = AI slop.
- **Native static recipe:** Visual that blends into the feed but visually contrasts (purple, medical, jarring) to stop the scroll. Primary text does the selling.
- **Hook test:** Find clips where mom or someone says "what the fuck was that?" — that's your scroll stopper.

## Counterpoints / caveats

- Don't jump on AI realism as your differentiator — that race is already over.
- Don't run new ad concepts without research. AI rewrites of competitor ads without foundational docs = slop.
- Native statics are running but trend awareness on Twitter/IG means the format's decline curve is starting.
- TikTok hooks ≠ direct-response hooks. Attention skills don't equal conversion skills.
- "Funnel diversity > creative diversity" is Mark's contrarian opinion — most marketers disagree.
