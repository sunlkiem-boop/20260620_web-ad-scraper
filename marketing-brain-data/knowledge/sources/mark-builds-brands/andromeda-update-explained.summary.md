# why your ads are performing like sh*t (urgent andromeda update)

## TL;DR
Andromeda is Meta's AI-powered retrieval algorithm (announced Dec 2024, fully live July 2025) that sorts tens of millions of ads in ~200 ms. The decisive change: instead of evaluating creatives one-by-one, Andromeda **groups similar-looking creatives together** and tests them as a cluster. That's why variation spamming has collapsed in effectiveness and creative diversity is the new moat. Mark flipped from 80% variations / 20% new concepts to 80% new concepts / 20% variations — and sometimes 100% new concepts.

## Frameworks & models

- **4-step delivery pipeline** (Andromeda specifically powers step 1):
  1. **Retrieval** — tens of millions of ads → thousands. *This is where Andromeda lives.*
  2. **Light ranking** — thousands → hundreds.
  3. **Final scoring** — ranked by Total Predicted Advertiser Value.
  4. **Auction** — competing for the impression.
- **Old vs new retrieval:** Old = manual rule-based filtering, ad-by-ad, on regular compute. New = single AI brain running on Nvidia Grace Hopper supercomputer chips, grouping ads into clusters of similar creatives.
- **Why Andromeda was built:** Meta admits ad volume hit "three orders of magnitude more than previous stages" due to AI-generated creative flooding the platform. The old system couldn't sort fast enough.
- **Three creative tiers (Mark's internal taxonomy):**
  - **New concept** — different format / angle / philosophy / avatar.
  - **Large variation** — copy AND image change, but structure stays the same.
  - **Small variation** — only image changes; copy identical.
- **Personalization > mass-market** under Andromeda. Targeting one specific avatar/archetype outperforms broad hero ads, even though placement targeting is fully broad.

## Key principles

- **The creative is the targeting.** Facebook's AI reads every visual element of your ad — skin tone, age, gender, background, mountains, flowers, sentiment of the copy — and that's what assigns who sees it.
- **Quality > quantity.** A good advertiser would rather have 20 unique knockout concepts than 300 near-identical variations.
- **You can load 25–50 ads per adset post-Andromeda** without breaking the algorithm — as long as they're truly diverse. Old "3–5 ads per adset" guidance is outdated.
- Media buying is now ~10–20% of total ad success (Mark says "and I'm being generous"). Creative + funnel are the rest.
- Targeting has been dying a slow death for years — Andromeda just made it official.
- "Most people just throw around 'creative diversity' without understanding *why* it matters." The why is the grouping mechanic.

## Tactics & playbook steps

1. **Shift the ratio.** Make 80% of your testing new concepts, 20% variations. New launches → 100% new concepts is fine.
2. **Define a new concept by:**
   - **Format change** (VSL → static image with long-form copy, → short-form podcast ad, etc.)
   - **Angle change** (different reason the customer buys)
   - **Avatar change** (different archetype targeted)
   - **Central philosophy change**
3. **Variation = small adjustments:** new hook on a video, new lead in a VSL, new thumbnail. Still allowed — just not your primary play.
4. **Load up adsets.** Where you used to put 3–5 creatives, you can now put 25–50 *diverse* creatives.
5. **Don't dump 50 near-identical variations into one adset** — Andromeda will group them into one and you'll waste spend.
6. **Diagnose if you're variation-testing:** if your 3 creatives share format, copy structure, and most of the image — you're variation testing. If image, copy, and structure all differ but the philosophy is shared — you're new-concept testing.

## Counterpoints / caveats

- Variations aren't *dead* — they still play a role *after* you find a winning concept.
- More ads per adset works *only if they're diverse.* Spamming similar variations into a fat adset will break performance.
- "Personalized over mass-market" still requires broad placement targeting — the personalization is in the creative, not the audience setup.
- "Most people don't know what they're talking about" re: Andromeda content on YouTube — Mark's commentary, not a verified claim about other creators.
