# how i build quiz funnels that make $1,073,966/mo with branded dropshipping

## TL;DR
Quiz funnels are doing ~$1M/month for Mark — but not for the reasons most people assume (personalization, product matching, AOV uplift). The real driver: putting the customer in an **emotionally primed state** before they ever see the offer. Every winning quiz funnel does three things: micro-commitments, seeding, and pre-handling objections. Quiz funnels are "the foreplay of funnels" — and they're a limited-window trend (advertorials → VSLs → quizzes is the lineage).

## Frameworks & models

- **The three pillars of a winning quiz funnel:**
  1. **Micro-commitments** — every yes/click/slider movement gets the customer one step closer to a sale. String enough together and you create a *hypnotic, highly suggestible state* before showing the offer.
  2. **Seeding** — subconsciously inserting beliefs into the customer's mind that align with buying. "Marketing at its core is a game of belief change."
  3. **Pre-handling objections** — adapted from a sales mentor: handle the objection inside the quiz before the offer is ever shown. The best copywriters don't do objection handling on the sales page because everything is handled before the offer reveals.
- **Gradualization** (Eugene Schwartz term). Start with non-invasive questions; escalate invasiveness slowly. Especially critical when selling to older women — never ask age first.
- **Foundational docs** as the input. Four docs: **Offer**, **Avatar**, **Research**, **Beliefs**. Built via AI in ~30 minutes using Mark's SOP (DM "foundation" on @markbuildsbrand IG).
- **Intra-quiz cards** — visual content blocks *between* questions used to insert beliefs and pre-handle objections (separate from the questions themselves).
- **Trend lineage:** Advertorials → VSLs → Quiz funnels. Each lasted for a window. Quiz funnels are the current window — "you have a very limited time to milk it for all that it's worth."

## Key principles

- The CTA "Take the quiz" has *significantly lower perceived invasiveness* than "Shop now" / "Buy now" — that's part of why quiz ads outperform direct-to-PDP traffic at the click stage.
- **Don't repeat the same question format more than 2–3 times in a row.** Mix multiple choice → slider → yes/no → multi-select. Prevents passive clicking and induces dopamine via UX variety.
- **Every question must do one of three jobs:** micro-commit, pre-handle an objection, or seed a belief. If it does none, cut it.
- **Avoid free-text input questions.** Typing is much higher perceived effort than selection — kills completion rate even if the question is good.
- **30% quiz completion rate is the KPI.** 25% is still good. Below that, find the high-drop-off question and cut or rephrase it.
- **8–12 questions is the sweet spot** (3–20 range). Too few = no priming. Too many = death by friction.
- **The first question is the most important.** Non-invasive, social-norm-respecting, and ideally seeds a belief (e.g. "biggest gut health goal" → seeds "gut health matters to mood/focus/energy").
- Adding a quiz period — even a basic 3-question one — almost always lifts conversion rate vs. direct-to-PDP traffic.
- Personalization on the *results page* isn't the conversion driver; emotional priming during the quiz is.

## Tactics & playbook steps — exact playbook

1. **Build foundational docs** (Offer / Avatar / Research / Beliefs) via the SOP, ~30 min in AI.
2. **Bootstrap the chat:** create a ChatGPT/Claude window, upload the four docs, prompt the model to take on context as a copywriter for the brand. Do not give action steps yet.
3. **Feed a competitor quiz:** screenshot every step of a successful competitor quiz (Mark uses Fioma as the example). Upload to the same chat. Have the model analyze.
4. **Generate your quiz** with a single prompt referencing the three pillars (micro-commit, seed, pre-handle) plus the gradualization rule and format-variety rule.
5. **Build the actual quiz with:**
   - Q1 = non-invasive, seeds the core belief about the problem area.
   - 2–3 question format switch every couple of questions.
   - Sliders to *measure intensity* of pain (seeds urgency — "this affects my quality of life").
   - Objection-pre-handler question (e.g. "how quickly do you expect results?" pre-handles "this takes too long").
   - Avoid free-text inputs.
   - End with **age range and weight goal** (or equivalent invasive Qs) — never up front.
6. **Capture name + email** at the end of the quiz, before showing results. They're maximally bought in at this point.
7. **Drop into 5–7 email sequence** post-quiz to close the sale.
8. **Branch email sequences by quiz answer** for the psycho mode: e.g. natural-ingredients seekers vs. speed-seekers get different copy angles.
9. **Analyze per-question drop-off** weekly. Look for the spike — that's a friction question to remove or rephrase.
10. **Launch lean, add qualifying friction later.** Don't load up friction upfront; first prove the quiz funnel works, then add questions that qualify buyer intent.
11. **CTA on ads:** "Take the quiz" (lower invasiveness) — not "Shop now."

## Counterpoints / caveats

- Quiz funnels are a *trend window*. The principle (emotional priming, blending in) is timeless, but the specific format will eventually saturate. Treat it as a current-meta play.
- AOV-lift claims for quiz funnels are mostly hype — Mark doesn't credit AOV improvement.
- Personalization on the results page is *not* the primary driver of conversions. Emotional priming through the quiz process is.
- Some questions improve completion rate by *adding* friction (qualifying intent). Don't blindly cut every friction question — only the ones with clear spikes that don't qualify the buyer.
