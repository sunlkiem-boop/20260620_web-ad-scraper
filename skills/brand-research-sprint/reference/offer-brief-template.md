# Offer Brief Template — Phase 2 Reference

Save as `<save-path>/<Brand>_<Product>_Offer_Brief.md` (+ matching `.pdf`).

**Target length:** 15+ PDF pages of dense content (roughly 550+ lines / 44KB+).

**Critical rule:** fill EVERY template field. Don't skip. Don't truncate. The brief is the campaign architecture — every blank field is a creative-direction question the brand will have to answer downstream.

## Required template fields (use these exact section headings)

### Potential Product Name Ideas

3–5 angle alternatives. Default stays the brand-locked name. List variant names as creative-side codenames (not for public rebrand).

```markdown
1. **<Product>** *(default — keep)*
2. **<Product> Daily** — sub-creative angle for the "every-morning ritual" frame
3. **<Product> 32** — sub-creative angle for the macro-tracker
4. **<Product> Clean** — sub-creative angle for the no-X-ingredient buyer
```

### Level of consciousness: LOW / HIGH

A 3–5 sentence assessment. RTD protein is HIGH (buyer already drinks shakes daily). NMN supplementation is LOW (buyer doesn't know NMN exists). Anchor with what the buyer already does, what target macros they're hitting, what competitor benchmark they default to.

End with **Implication for creative:** [1 sentence — what this means for headline strategy].

### Level of Awareness

Eugene Schwartz's 5 awareness levels mapped to the avatars from `<Brand>_<Product>_Avatar_Sheets.md` in a 3-column table:

| Avatar | Schwartz Level | Where they are mentally |
|---|---|---|
| Avatar 1 | [Unaware / Problem-Aware / Solution-Aware / Product-Aware / Most-Aware] | [1-sentence description] |
| Avatar 2 | [level] | [description] |
| Avatar 3 | [level] | [description] |

End with **Implication for creative:** [1 sentence].

### Stage of Sophistication: LEVEL N

Eugene Schwartz 1–5. For each level, write the 1-sentence rule. Then explain where this product sits, what works at this level, and what does NOT work. End with **Implication for creative.**

For Level 4–5 markets (most CPG categories), the standard moves are:
1. Identification copy — show the customer themselves and validate their journey
2. Earned authority — specific credentials, not generic "Dr. recommended"
3. A new enemy, not a new feature
4. Risk reversal that doesn't reset

### Big Idea

A blockquote sentence (the H1 for every long-form ad). One sentence that articulates the brand's position vs. the category enemy. Example format:

> **"[The category] was hijacked by [the villain / compromise]. [Brand] refuses. [Key product fact] — [in a format / way the customer doesn't have to apologize for]."**

Follow with 3 numbered "why this Big Idea is right" anchors (each 1–2 sentences).

### Metaphor

A blockquote one-liner that distills the Big Idea into a visual / metaphor. Example format:

> **"Most [category] are [negative thing]. [Brand] is [the thing they wish it was]."**

Follow with a "supporting metaphor stack" (4–6 alt one-liners the creative team can A/B test).

### Potential UMP (unique mechanism of the problem)

**The root cause the product exposes.** Structure as 2–4 layered sub-arguments:

#### Layer 1 — [root cause name]
[1 paragraph + verbatim quote from VOC §3 as evidence]

#### Layer 2 — [contributing cause name]
[1 paragraph + quote evidence]

#### Layer 3 — [secondary cause name]
[1 paragraph + quote evidence]

End with **UMP one-liner:** *"[one-sentence summary of the entire UMP frame]"*

### Potential UMS (unique mechanism of the solution)

**The proprietary mechanism — 2–4 layers mapped to the §0.4 wedges in the VOC dossier.**

Same structure as UMP, but the layers are the product's mechanisms. Include any technical / biological reasoning, a comparison table where it helps, and authority quotes from named experts.

Example UMS comparison table format (Layer 3):

```markdown
| Brand | Protein (g) | Calories | cal/g protein |
|---|---|---|---|
| **<Product>** | **N** | **N** | **N (best in class)** |
| Competitor 1 | N | N | N |
| ...
```

End with **UMS one-liner:** *"[one-sentence summary]"*

### Guru

3–4 named credential-bearing humans associated with the brand. For each:

- Name + title
- Credentials (degree / institution / public-figure following / book authorship)
- Verbatim public-facing quote that installs the brand belief
- **Where this person works:** which avatar(s) they're most credible to

End with press credibility (one-liner: "Forbes · People Magazine · Yahoo Life · AthleTech News" or whatever the brand has).

### Discovery Story

A blockquote 2–4 paragraph narrative — the brand's origin story arc. Defensible scaffold built from public sources.

**End with a verification flag:** *"Status of this story: Inferable from public sources... Verification flag: the brand may have an internal origin story (a specific tasting session, a specific customer interview, a specific founder moment) that should be slotted in where I've used the generic frame. Update before deploying as ad copy."*

### Product

Exhaustive spec. Every macro, every ingredient, every certification, every claim:

- Name
- Format (container, size, pack-size)
- Channel
- Price (on sale, full, $/unit)
- Flavors
- Macros per unit (verify against label)
- Active ingredient source
- Sweetener system (verify; flag any discrepancy with brand claims)
- Stabilizer / inactive ingredients (verify; flag any discrepancy)
- Made in
- Co-formulated by
- Subscription / risk-reversal framing
- What it's NOT

### Potential Headline / Subheadline Ideas

**8–12 VOC-anchored candidates.** Tag the strongest 4–5 with ⭐.

Group by angle (1) Sucralose-wedge / category-enemy / 2) Macro-math / 3) Brand-mission / 4) Doctor-credibility / 5) Big-Idea hybrid).

Each headline is a numbered entry:

```markdown
1. ⭐ **"<Headline>"**
   *Sub: "<Subheadline>"*
   *[Optional: 1-sentence note on compliance / channel suitability — e.g., "Compliance note: direct competitor naming. Use cautiously — only on owned channels, not on Meta Ads. Better as advertorial / long-form."]*
```

### List all objections (and answer scripts)

**Every objection the buyer will raise — even uncomfortable ones.** For each:

```markdown
### O[N]. *"[Customer voice version of the objection]"*

**Customer voice:** *"[the canonical VOC §3 quote that proves the objection exists]"*
**Answer:** *"[1–3 sentence answer script using brand-approved language — preserves required anchors like 'scientifically studied dosages' and 'no pixie-dusting']"*
```

Target 10–15 objections. Common categories:
- Price / value
- Ingredient skepticism (sucralose, carrageenan, etc.)
- Taste / texture
- Format
- Distribution (why isn't this at Costco?)
- Founder skepticism (is this just an influencer brand?)
- Risk-reversal (no refunds?)
- Competitor comparison

### Belief Chain — 10-step granular journey from cold to buyer

10 numbered beliefs the prospect must hold or be moved to hold before they convert. Each:

```markdown
**Belief N — [stage]:** *"[customer-voice version of the belief]"*
**Move:** [1 sentence — what creative move installs this belief]. *Creative: "[example headline / hook / proof point that delivers the move]"*
```

This 10-step chain compresses into the Phase-3 Beliefs Doc's 6 meta-beliefs.

### Funnel Architecture

3 tiers:

#### Top of funnel — paid cold traffic
- **Channels:** [Meta + TikTok + YouTube + podcast pre-rolls — be specific about which ad inventory]
- **Creative angles:** 4 numbered hook approaches
- **Landing destination:** advertorial or product page?

#### Mid-funnel — site visitors + warm retargeting
- **Channels:**
- **Creative angles:** 3–4 retargeting approaches

#### Bottom of funnel — high-intent / cart abandoners / subscription drop-offs
- **Creative angles:** 3–4 conversion-completion approaches

#### Email flow (post-purchase)
6-touch post-purchase sequence. Day 1 → Day 30. Each touch named.

### Potential domains

5–8 candidate domains for campaign landing pages / advertorial URLs / partner campaigns. Default stays brand main domain.

Note availability constraint: "Verify domain availability before purchase. Default cleanest stays [brand-main-domain]."

### Examples / Swipes

Real ads observed across the comp set, useful as creative reference points to **imitate-or-differentiate**.

Sub-sections:
- Creative pattern to **lift from** (and which competitor exemplifies)
- Creative pattern to **differentiate from** (and why)
- Specific ad-copy swipes to test (verbatim VOC sentences as headlines)

### Other notes

Wide grab-bag. Include:

#### Creative golden rules
The 5–8 most important DON'Ts for this brand — including specific ingredient or claim self-owns (e.g. "do NOT attack [ingredient X] if the product also contains it — verified against §0").

#### Pricing-strategy notes
The price-to-feel anchor, subscription path to LTV, bundle strategy.

#### Brand-ecosystem notes
How this SKU connects to the rest of the brand line. Cross-sell opportunities.

#### Compliance + brand-voice red lines (one-screen reference)

A 2-column table: tempting phrase → replace-with.

#### Things to verify with the brand before final ad copy

5–8 numbered verification flags. The Discovery Story specifics, subscription pricing floor, retail roadmap, on-camera asset availability, etc.
